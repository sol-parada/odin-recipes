Milanesa Recipe

Description:

Milanesa are a traditional Argentine dish made of thin slices of meat (usually beef or chicken) breaded and fried until golden and crispy. Similar to a schnitzel, they are a staple in Argentine households and often served with mashed potatoes, fries, or salad.

Ingredients (Serves 4)

  • 4 beef cutlets
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cloves of garli
  • 1 tablespoon chopped par
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 1/2 cups bread
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps:

  1. Prepare the egg mix
    • In a shallow bowl, beat the eggs.
    • Add the minced garlic, chopped parsley (if using), salt, and pepper.
    • Mix well.
  2. Prepare the breadcrumb mixture:
    • In another shallow dish, mix the breadcrumbs with the grated PArmesan (if using)
  3. Bread the beef:
    • Dip each beef cutlet into the egg mixture, coating both sides.
    • Then coat it with the breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently so the crumbs stick well.
    • Place the breaded cutlets on a plate or tray.
  4. Let it rest (optional but recommended)
    • Let the breaded cutlets rest in the fridge for 15-30 minutes. This helps the coating stick better during frying.
  5. Fry milanesas:
    • In a large skillet, heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
    • Fry the milanesas one or two at time (don't overcrowd the pan) until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
    • Remove and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve hot with mashed potatoes, rice, salad, or in a sandwich (milanesa sandwich!).
